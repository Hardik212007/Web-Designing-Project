Glossary

  • Oil Painting: A painting technique that uses pigments mixed with oil, typically linseed oil, to create rich and vibrant colors.
  • Surrealism: An artistic movement that seeks to express the unconscious mind through dream-like and fantastical imagery.
  • Expressionism: An art movement that emphasizes the expression of emotional experience over physical reality, often through bold colors and distorted forms.
  • Portrait Painting: A genre of painting that focuses on depicting a person or group of people, capturing their likeness and personality.
  • Anti-War Painting: Artworks created to protest war and convey the horrors and consequences of conflict.

    2. The Starry Night

    by Vincent van Gogh

    Description: The Starry Night is one of Vincent van Gogh's most famous paintings, depicting a night sky over a quiet town.
    The painting was created in mid-June 1889, inspired by the view from Van Gogh's bedroom window at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum.
    The former monastery functioned as a mental asylum, where Van Gogh voluntarily admitted himself on 8 May 1889, following a mental breakdown and his infamous act of self-mutilation that occurred in late December 1888.

    Mona Lisa

    by Leonardo da Vinci

    Description: The Mona Lisa is a portrait painting by the renowned artist Leonardo da Vinci.
    The painting has been traditionally considered to depict the Italian noblewoman Lisa del Giocondo.
    It is painted in oil on a white poplar panel.Leonardo never gave the painting to the Giocondo family.It was believed to have been painted between 1503 and 1506.

    Know more about The Mona Lisa

    The Persistence of Memory

    by Salvador Dalí

    Description: The Persistence of Memory is a surrealist painting by Salvador Dalí, famous for its melting clocks.
    The well-known surrealist piece introduced the image of the soft melting pocket watch.
    It epitomizes Dalí's theory of "softness" and "hardness", which was central to his thinking at the time.

    Know more about The Persistence of Memory

    The Scream

    by Edvard Munch

    Description: The Scream is an iconic expressionist painting by Edvard Munch, representing a figure with an agonized expression against a tumultuous sky.
    The agonized face in the painting has become one of the most iconic images in art, seen as representing a profound experience of existential dread related to the human condition.
    Munch's work, including The Scream, had a formative influence on the Expressionist movement.

    Guernica

    by Picasso

    Description: Guernica is a large 1937 oil painting by Spanish artist Pablo Picasso.It is one of his best-known works, regarded by many art critics as the most moving and powerful anti-war painting in history.It is exhibited in the Museo Reina Sofía in Madrid.
